# AI in the Office, with Guardrails

A practical guide to applying **Spec-Driven Development (SDD)** to everyday office work with AI.

This repository is for teams who use tools like **ChatGPT** or **Claude** outside the IDE for tasks such as:

- reviewing contracts
- extracting key points from documents
- standardising repeatable admin workflows
- processing image batches with consistent rules
- drafting structured prompts for operational use

The core idea is simple:

> Before asking AI to do the task, define the task clearly enough that it can be repeated, reviewed, and trusted.

---

## Why this repo exists

Most AI advice is either:

- too generic
- too technical
- too focused on software engineering workflows

This repo takes the useful part of **Spec-Driven Development** and translates it into something lighter and more practical for **office-style work**.

That means turning vague requests like:

- “crop these images”
- “summarise this contract”
- “find the important parts”

into reusable prompt structures with:

- clear goals
- defined inputs
- expected outputs
- rules
- exceptions
- review standards

---

## What “SDD” means here

In this repository, **SDD** does not mean heavyweight process.

It means using a small spec before execution.

A good AI task usually becomes:

1. **Goal** — what the task is
2. **Inputs** — what the AI will receive
3. **Output** — what it must return
4. **Rules** — what must always happen
5. **Exceptions** — what to do when input is incomplete or messy
6. **Definition of done** — what counts as complete

This reduces ambiguity, improves consistency, and makes prompts easier to share across a team.

---

## Important note on governance

AI can improve speed and clarity, but it does **not** replace company policy, legal review, or human judgement.

Before using AI with real business materials:

- confirm what your company allows you to upload
- avoid sharing confidential or regulated information unless approved
- treat AI output as a draft
- keep human review for legal, HR, financial, and customer-facing decisions
- separate factual extraction from interpretation
